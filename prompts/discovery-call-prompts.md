# Discovery Call Prompts

Prompts for before, during prep, and after discovery calls. The call itself is yours; AI does the prep and the debrief.

**The truth about discovery:** deals are won or lost here, not at the demo. If you leave a discovery call without knowing the prospect's problem, its cost, who decides, and what happens if they do nothing, you don't have a deal. You have a chat.

---

## 1. Pre-call research and battle plan

```
I have a discovery call with [name], [title] at [company] in [industry, company size].
I sell [product, one sentence]. Typical value we deliver: [outcome].

Context on how the call got booked: [inbound demo request / replied to cold email / referral / etc.]

Here is what I know about them:
[paste LinkedIn, website notes, any emails exchanged]

Build me a battle plan:
1. Three hypotheses about why they took this call, ranked by likelihood
2. The 5 discovery questions most likely to surface real pain, tailored to their role
3. Likely objections and what each would really mean
4. What a "qualified" outcome looks like vs. a polite waste of 30 minutes
5. One insight about their industry or situation I can share to earn credibility early
```

---

## 2. Question bank generator

```
I sell [product] to [buyer role]. The problems we solve: [list 2-3].

Generate discovery questions in these categories, 4 each:
1. Current state ("how do you handle X today?")
2. Pain and impact ("what does that cost you?" in time, money, risk, morale)
3. Decision process (who else, what budget, what timeline, what alternatives)
4. Consequence of inaction ("what happens if this stays the same for a year?")

Rules:
- Open questions only, no yes/no
- No jargon, no leading questions that telegraph my pitch
- Phrase them the way a curious peer would ask, not an interrogator
- Mark the 3 questions that do the most work if I only get 15 minutes
```

---

## 3. Call debrief and next-step email

Paste your notes or the transcript right after the call, while it's fresh.

```
Here are my notes/transcript from a discovery call with [name] at [company]:

[paste notes or transcript]

1. Summarize: their problem, its cost, who decides, timeline, and what they said about budget
2. Qualification check: score this deal on pain, urgency, authority, and fit (1-5 each) with evidence from the call. Be harsh.
3. What did I fail to ask? List the gaps.
4. Red flags I might be ignoring because I want this deal
5. Draft a follow-up email: 3 sentences summarizing what I heard, 1 confirming the agreed next step with a date. No pitch, no fluff.
```

---

## 4. Deal review (use weekly on every open deal)

```
Act as my sales manager. Here is the state of a deal:

Company: [name, size, industry]
Contact: [name, title] - is this the decision maker? [yes/no/unclear]
Their problem, in their words: [quote or summary]
What it costs them: [known / unknown]
Next step agreed: [what and when, or "none"]
How long in pipeline: [weeks]
Last contact: [date]

Interrogate this deal:
1. Is this a real deal or am I fooling myself? Verdict first, then reasoning.
2. The single biggest risk to it closing
3. The one action this week that most improves my position
4. What I should ask next time that I clearly haven't asked yet
```

---

## 5. Roleplay a hard prospect

Practice before important calls. This works scarily well.

```
Roleplay with me. You are [title] at [company type]. Your personality: skeptical, busy, has been burned by vendors before. You have the problem my product solves ([problem]) but you don't believe tools fix it, and you didn't ask for this call - your CEO told you to take it.

I am the founder selling [product]. Start the call by saying "So... what's this about? I have a hard stop in 20."

Stay in character. Make me work for every piece of information. If I pitch too early, get visibly bored. If I ask a good question, open up slightly. After 10 exchanges, break character and give me brutal feedback on what I did wrong.
```

---

## Field notes

- **Talk ratio:** you should talk 30% of a discovery call, max. If you demo'd anything in the first 15 minutes, you lost.
- **"That's interesting, tell me more"** is the highest-ROI sentence in sales.
- **Always book the next step on the call.** "I'll send some times" is where deals go to die.
- **Silence is a tool.** Ask the cost question, then shut up. The first number they say is information you can't get any other way.
