# Founder-Led Sales: The Playbook

For technical and product founders who'd rather do anything else. Bad news first: you can't skip this stage. Good news: you have advantages no salesperson will ever have, and the whole thing is more learnable than you think.

---

## Why you can't hire your way out yet

Before ~€500K ARR (or ~20 closed deals, whichever framing helps), hiring a salesperson to "handle sales" fails for a predictable reason: **there's nothing to hand over.** No proven pitch, no known buyer, no repeatable process. A salesperson executes a playbook; they don't write one. You're asking someone with less product knowledge, less credibility, and less motivation than you to figure out what you couldn't.

The failure pattern is always the same: hire an experienced AE, wait 2 quarters, blame the hire, repeat. That's 6-12 months and €100K+ learning what this paragraph just told you. More detail in [Hiring your first salesperson](./hiring-your-first-salesperson.md).

## Your unfair advantages

Founders consistently underrate these:

1. **You can say "I built this."** Buyers take calls with founders they'd never take with SDRs. Use the title shamelessly.
2. **You can change the product.** "We don't do that, but we could by March" is a sentence no AE can say.
3. **You don't need permission to walk away.** No quota panic. You can qualify honestly, and honesty closes.
4. **Nobody knows the problem better.** Discovery calls where the seller deeply understands the domain feel like consulting. Prospects pay for consulting.

Your disadvantage is one: you take rejection personally, because the product is you. The fix is volume. Rejection number 40 hurts a tenth of rejection number 4.

## The weekly operating system

Sales fails for founders through inconsistency, not incompetence. Two weeks of enthusiasm, then a product fire, then an empty pipeline in month 3 - because pipeline is a lagging indicator of prospecting you did 6 weeks ago.

The minimum viable sales week, ~8 hours:

- **3 hours prospecting** (non-negotiable, calendar-blocked, mornings): 15-20 researched outreaches using the [cold outreach prompts](../prompts/cold-outreach-prompts.md)
- **3 hours calls:** discovery and follow-ups, using the [discovery framework](../templates/discovery-call-framework.md)
- **1 hour follow-up:** every promised email out within 24h, sequences running per the [follow-up prompts](../prompts/follow-up-prompts.md)
- **30 min Monday pipeline review** with the [pipeline template](../templates/sales-pipeline-stages.md)
- **30 min learning:** debrief your worst call of the week, not your best

Protect the prospecting block like you'd protect a production deploy. It's the one that quietly dies.

## The mindset shifts that matter

**From pitching to diagnosing.** You're not convincing anyone. You're finding people who already have the problem and helping them see its cost clearly. The best sales call feels, to the buyer, like a great consulting session.

**From features to before/after.** Nobody buys features. They buy their Tuesday looking different: fewer angry emails, a report done in minutes, a boss off their back. Sell the after-state.

**From "closing" to "deciding."** Your job at every stage is getting a decision, and "no" is the second-best answer. The enemy is "maybe" - maybes eat your calendar and your morale.

**From artisanal to instrumented.** You A/B test features; A/B test your pitch. Track reply rates and stage conversions. When something's off, the numbers tell you where. Sales is a funnel, and you already know how to think about funnels.

## Using AI without sounding like AI

The prompts in this repo automate research, drafts, prep, and analysis. Deliberately not automated: talking to humans. The rule that keeps you safe: **AI writes the first draft, you write the last one.** Personalization at scale is only leverage if the judgment layer stays human.

## When it's working

You'll know founder-led sales has done its job when: you've closed 15-20 deals yourself, the same pitch works repeatedly, you can predict which prospects will buy, and your close rate from qualified conversations is stable. That's not the finish line of sales. That's the starting line for [hiring](./hiring-your-first-salesperson.md).
